SINCE 1930.

NAME AFTER A HORSE.

Snickers was named after the favorite horse of the Mars family. Frank Mars, the founder of Mars, Incorporated, named the candy bar after his horse, which was named Snickers.

Main INGREDIENTS.

Snickers consists of nougat topped with caramel and peanuts, and covered in milk chocolate.

VARIETY.

Over the years, Snickers has expanded its product line to include variations such as Snickers Almond, Snickers Peanut Butter, Snickers Crisper, and Snickers Ice Cream.

NUTRITIONAL IMPACT.

Snickers contains carbohydrates for quick energy from the sugar and protein from the peanuts, making it a popular choice for a quick snack.

MARS INCORPORATED.

Snickers is manufactured by Mars, Incorporated, a global company known for its confectionery, food, and pet care products.

ADVERTISING CAMPAIGNS.

Snickers has been known for its memorable advertising campaigns, including the famous "You're Not You When You're Hungry" campaign, featuring celebrities in humorous situations.

NEUTRIENTS

A standard Snickers bar contains approximately 215 calories per serving. Each serving also includes 11.5 grams of total fat, with 4.5 grams of saturated fat. There's a cholesterol content of 5 milligrams and a sodium content of 86 milligrams. In terms of carbohydrates, each serving contains about 27.5 grams, including 1 gram of dietary fiber and 22.5 grams of sugars. Additionally, Snickers bars provide approximately 3.5 grams of protein per serving. It's important to note that these values may vary slightly depending on the specific ingredients and recipe used by the manufacturer. As with any food product, moderation is key when consuming Snickers bars to maintain a balanced diet.
